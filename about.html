For nearly four decades, Quorena Root Haven has been more than a garden — it’s a living testament to regenerative agriculture, community cultivation, and the sacred bond between human hands and the brown earth.

Our Genesis: A Seed Planted in 1987

Quorena Root Haven was born from a vision: to create a sanctuary where organic gardening, ecological wisdom, and human connection intertwine. Founders Margaret and Elias Quorena, both botanists disillusioned by industrial agriculture, purchased a worn-out farmland in Quorena Valley. The soil was depleted, the land eroded, but they saw potential. With nothing but hand tools, heirloom seeds, and a deep reverence for nature, they began the slow work of restoration. They planted windbreaks, built compost piles from fallen leaves, and nurtured the microbial life beneath their feet. By 1992, the land transformed into a lush demonstration site for organic methods, attracting neighbors and fellow gardeners. What started as a family homestead evolved into a nonprofit educational hub, welcoming thousands each year to learn the art of earth-centered horticulture.

🌿 The Root Haven Philosophy — Beyond Organic

“Beyond organic” means we don’t just avoid chemicals — we actively regenerate. We view the garden as a holistic organism: soil fungi, pollinators, wild edges, and human stewards form one network. Our practices synthesize permaculture ethics (Earth Care, People Care, Fair Share) with traditional farming techniques passed down through generations. Every decision, from the brown cedar trellises to the rain-fed swales, reflects a commitment to biodiversity and climate resilience. We’ve also pioneered the “Root Haven Method”: a no-till system that combines deep mulch, green manure, and perennial polycultures. The result is not only abundant harvests but also carbon-sequestering soils and a haven for native wildlife.

From a Homestead to a Global Inspiration

Today, Quorena Root Haven spans 47 acres of diverse ecosystems: food forests, medicinal herb spiral gardens, intensive vegetable terraces, a native prairie restoration zone, and an educational campus with demonstration greenhouses. Annually, we host over 5,000 visitors through workshops, farm stays, and our renowned “Earth Apprentice” program. Our digital library (including the extensive compendium featured on our homepage) reaches gardeners in 90+ countries. We’ve also partnered with schools to install learning gardens and with urban farms to share techniques for small-space abundance. Despite our growth, the spirit remains intimate: you can still find Margaret pruning the heritage apple trees at dawn, and Elias teaching compost microbiology with contagious enthusiasm.

Our Land & Its Living Legacy

The Quorena Valley itself holds deep history — originally stewarded by Indigenous peoples who cultivated native plants and managed the woodlands for millennia. We honor this legacy by incorporating traditional ecological knowledge (TEK) into our practices: companion planting based on Three Sisters (corn, beans, squash), controlled burns for prairie health, and wildcrafting ethics. In 2015, we established the “Brown Earth Reparations Fund”, supporting Indigenous-led land restoration projects. The land now boasts over 200 species of native plants, a restored wetland that filters runoff, and soil organic matter increased from 1.2% to 8.7% since 1987 — a testament to what regenerative care can achieve.

What Makes Us Unique: The Root Haven Experience

Unlike conventional gardens or even standard organic farms, Quorena Root Haven invites visitors to not just see but participate. From barefoot soil-sensing walks to mushroom inoculation workshops, we emphasize sensory connection. The “Root Immersion” residency allows guests to live on-site for a week, engaging in daily garden tasks, harvesting meals from the earth, and learning craftsmanship like building with cob or crafting willow trellises. Our seed library, one of the largest in the region, holds over 800 varieties of rare and heritage seeds, available freely to low-income growers. We also run a therapeutic horticulture program for veterans and individuals recovering from trauma — the brown earth, we believe, heals as much as it feeds.

Community Impact & Outreach

Beyond our fences, Root Haven has helped establish 17 community gardens across the county, donating tools, soil, and starter plants. Our “Gardens for All” initiative distributes thousands of vegetable seedlings to food deserts each spring. We also maintain a sliding-scale CSA (Community Supported Agriculture) that makes fresh organic produce accessible to families of all incomes. In partnership with regional universities, we host the annual "Brown Earth Symposium" — a gathering of soil scientists, activists, and farmers reimagining food systems. Through it all, we remain guided by a simple belief: when you heal the earth, you heal community.

Looking Forward: The Next Chapter

As climate challenges intensify, Quorena Root Haven is expanding our work: we’re currently developing a 10-acre agroforestry research hub, trialing drought-resilient perennial grains and new silvopasture models. We’ve also launched the “Root Haven Media” platform to produce free documentaries and skill-share videos, ensuring that regenerative knowledge transcends borders. In the next five years, we aim to train 1,000 new regenerative farmers through our apprenticeship program, creating a ripple effect of ecological restoration. Our vision is unwavering: a world where every community has access to fertile soil, clean water, and the timeless art of gardening.

Whether you are a novice planting your first basil seedling or a seasoned grower, you are family here. Quorena Root Haven exists as a living library — come wander our pathways, sink your hands into the earth, and become part of this ever-unfolding story.
