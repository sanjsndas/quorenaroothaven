Privacy Policy — Nurturing Your Data with Care

🌼 Last revised: March 27, 2026 | Version 3.1 – “Soil & Soul Protection”

At Quorena Root Haven (“we,” “us,” “our”), your privacy is as essential as healthy topsoil. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, protect, and respect your personal information when you visit our website, register for workshops, purchase digital garden guides, join our Root Keeper community, or interact with our services. We are committed to transparency and honoring the trust you place in us — just like a garden thrives only when the grower’s stewardship is genuine.

1. Information We Collect — What Grows in Our Garden

We collect different types of information to enrich your experience, support our educational mission, and maintain the integrity of our community.

  • 🌿 Personal Information You Provide: When you sign up for newsletters, purchase a workshop, create a forum account, or request resources, we may collect: name, email address, postal address (for seed shipments or event materials), phone number, payment details (processed securely via third-party gateways), and any gardening preferences you choose to share.
  • 📝 Community Content: If you post comments, upload garden photos, share stories or testimonials, we store that content to facilitate community interactions. Always be mindful of sharing personal details publicly.
  • 🍪 Automatically Collected Data: Like most digital gardens, we gather technical information such as IP address, browser type, device identifiers, pages visited, and referral sources through cookies and similar technologies. This helps us improve site navigation and understand which heirloom articles resonate most.
  • 🌻 Event & Workshop Data: For in-person or online gatherings, we may collect attendance records, dietary restrictions (if refreshments are offered), and emergency contact details to ensure safety.

2. How We Use Your Information — Tending the Roots

We use the collected data solely for legitimate purposes tied to our gardening mission:

  • Provide, operate, and improve our Services, including personalized recommendations for upcoming workshops or planting calendars.
  • Process transactions and deliver digital products (e-books, course access) or physical seed packs.
  • Respond to inquiries, send service notifications, and share seasonal gardening tips with your consent.
  • Analyze usage trends to create better content — from mulching guides to pollinator conservation.
  • Protect our community from fraudulent activity, spam, or harmful behavior.
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service.
🌾 Your Growth, Our Promise: We never sell your personal data to third parties. Any sharing is limited to trusted partners (e.g., payment processors, mailing platforms) who adhere to strict confidentiality and data protection standards.

3. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR & Global Standards)

For individuals in the European Economic Area (EEA) or regions with similar data protection frameworks, our processing is based on: your consent (e.g., marketing communications), performance of a contract (workshop enrollment or purchases), compliance with legal obligations, and legitimate interests (improving our educational content). You have the right to withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

4. Cookies & Similar Technologies — Sowing Seeds of Convenience

We use essential cookies to ensure the site functions (like remembering your cart or login status). Functional and analytical cookies help us understand which articles inspire our readers. You can manage cookie preferences through our cookie consent tool or browser settings. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect site functionality. For full details, see our separate 🍪 Cookies Policy.

5. Sharing Your Information — Only When the Ecosystem Benefits

We may share information in limited scenarios:

  • Service Providers: Trusted third parties who assist with payment processing, email distribution (Mailchimp or similar), website analytics, and event registration. They are contractually bound to use data only for specified purposes.
  • Legal Compliance: If required by law, court order, or governmental regulation, we may disclose information to protect our rights, safety, or the rights of our community members.
  • Business Transitions: In the unlikely event of a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, your data may be transferred with notice and continued protection under this Privacy Policy.
  • With Your Consent: When you explicitly agree to share testimonials or garden stories in our newsletters or social media channels.

6. Data Security — Protecting the Living Soil

We implement industry-standard safeguards — encryption (SSL/TLS), secure server infrastructure, and restricted access — to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Our team is trained in data protection best practices. However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure; we encourage you to use strong, unique credentials for your account.

7. Your Rights & Choices — Pruning with Precision

Depending on your location (including under GDPR, CCPA, and similar laws), you may have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): Request deletion of your data, subject to legal or contractual retention obligations.
  • Restrict Processing: Limit how we use your information in certain circumstances.
  • Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Opt-Out of Marketing: Unsubscribe from newsletters via the link in any email or by contacting us directly.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Root Keeper Data Steward at privacy@quorenaroothaven.com. We will respond within 30 days (or as required by applicable law). We never discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights.

8. Children’s Privacy — Cultivating Young Gardeners Safely

Our Services are intended for a general audience; however, we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent. If you believe a child has provided us with data without consent, please contact us, and we will delete such information promptly. Family-friendly workshops always require guardian registration and supervision.

9. International Data Transfers — Seeds Crossing Borders

Quorena Root Haven is based in the Quorena Valley region. If you access our website from outside this jurisdiction, your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in countries where data protection laws may differ. We take steps to ensure adequate safeguards (such as Standard Contractual Clauses) are in place to protect your data consistent with this Privacy Policy.

10. Data Retention — Honoring the Seasonal Cycle

We retain personal data only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required by law. For example, transaction records are kept for tax/accounting obligations (typically 7 years), while forum posts remain until you delete your account. You may request deletion of inactive accounts at any time.

11. Third-Party Links — Exploring Neighboring Meadows

Our content may include links to partner seed suppliers, gardening tool artisans, or environmental organizations. Those third-party websites have their own privacy policies, and we are not responsible for their practices. We encourage you to read their policies before sharing any personal data.

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy — Pruning for Fresh Growth

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices, technology, or legal obligations. The “Last revised” date at the top indicates when the latest version took effect. For material changes, we will notify you via email (if we have your contact) or a prominent notice on our website. Your continued use of our Services after the update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
